Summer 2023

Experience with journalism

I spent my summer of 2023 at Harvard, studying media, political science, and journalism. Admittedly, the program itself wasn't too rigorous - but I fell so in love with journalism that I made it an intense summer. I studied with the Washington Editor at USA Today, and he was and still is one of my greatest mentors. I had been writing literary analysis pieces almost every other week since I was in 8th grade, so writing was something I found came to me fairly naturally. I wrote pieces on demographic inequality, on media releases, and personal profiles on people in Cambridge with unique backgrounds under his supervision, and by the end we both discussed the possibility of me entering the field.

The narrative journalism I experienced there was fascinating to me. Reading articles that told stories rather than simply presenting information — stories of real people to present data that otherwise feels inhumane and stale — this felt like what I was called to do.

I have my reasons now for choosing not to pursue that path, but it was a time that helped me realized the importance of stories and authenticity. It translates to the work I do now, and I still love writing.
