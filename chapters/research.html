College

Academia

I made the call to go to Swarthmore College despite my other options, ones that are better known name-wise, because I had no idea what I wanted to do with my life.

I knew that going to a liberal arts school, I would at least be able to explore everything that interested me - and I was interested in a whole lot of things. Swarthmore gave me the freedom to explore to my heart's content. I took random courses like engineering design, and I even went pretty far with math. What I ended up falling in love the most with was political philosophy. I learned modern political theory, democratic theory, tech philosophy, and global politics, and it didn't feel like work or school anymore. I'd enjoyed math and English in high school, but never had I fallen in love with learning as I did in these courses. Every reading was fascinating to me in ways I couldn't quite explain.

I understand that many people that come to Swarthmore are dissatisfied due to the weak social scene, the lack of support for employment in non-specialized industries, the location and cliqueyness, but none of those cons compared to the tingling excitement I would get as I indulged in learning. Going to a liberal arts school means that you have the privilege of an environment buzzing with intellectual hunger and engagement. You get to practice 'learning for the sake of learning.' I have considered pursuing a path in academia, and part of me still wants that; it is, at the very least, a possibility that I hold somewhere in my heart.
