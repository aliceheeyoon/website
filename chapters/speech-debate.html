2022-2024

Relationship with language

When I moved to Korea at the age of 7 from Connecticut, I barely knew any Korean. It was of survival instinct that I learned as quickly as I did when I was put into completely Korean-speaking schools. In just a few years, I read up and watched enough TV that I was pretty much fluent in both languages. I even got recognized for being a good writer in elementary school, and yes, I was on the classic kumon route which definitely helped too.

I actually had a phase where I would only let myself speak the language that I would be using, because I've always had a fear of being misunderstood or being perceived wrongly. When I was at my Korean music school, I refused to speak English out of insecurity and extremity, and also a desire to develop my eloquence as much as possible. I studied and memorized complex sayings called 사자성어. Then, when I went to my international school, everything had to flip. I stopped speaking Korean, I read as many books as I could (only in English), and I was still insecure about that side of my identity.

When one of my best friends invited me to join them at their speech and debate conference, I don't know what it was that drew me in. At the time, I resonated a lot with Min Jin Lee's "Breaking my silence" - maybe it was that. Either way, despite my shaking anxiety before the competition, I made my way to becoming a finalist in my speech category. I didn't continue pursuing speech and debate for long because of how busy I got with cello and other academics, but this was a pivotal time for me where I truly gained confidence in presenting what I deem is important and truthful, storytelling, and even writing.
